Sara Viloria

ColoristPantone CertifiedVisual ArtistAuthor

Sara Viloria (1991) holds a degree in Visual Arts and a diploma in Illustration and Territorial Art Expression. She is a Certified Colorist trained by Leatrice Eiseman, Executive Director of the Pantone Institute.

Painter, poet and writer — winner of the Neruda Foundation Grant (2021) and the Lily Peter Fellowship (USA). Author of: Color y Acuarela and Cartas para Siena.

CONFERENCE TOPICS

What We Learned

🎨

Color Theory

Color is a capricious language. It generates conscious, subconscious, and unconscious responses in the viewer.

🔵

Color Semiology

Every color carries a surname (a memory) and a sensation. The study of color signs reveals how societies assign meaning to visual stimuli.

🟡

Pantone System

The standardized color identification system used globally in fashion, design, and digital interfaces.

🌡️

Color Temperature

Colors are divided into warm and cool. Temperature affects emotional perception and spatial depth.

💫

Color Saturation

Saturation radiates outward from a central gray axis, indicating color intensity.

🎯

Color Harmonies

Complementary, triadic, analogous — harmony structures guide all visual design decisions.

PERSONAL REFLECTION

My Experience at Moda ECCI

Attending Moda ECCI was a transformative academic experience. Sara Viloria's conference opened my eyes to the deep connection between visual perception and human emotion. Color is not merely an aesthetic element — it is a language that speaks directly to our subconscious.

What struck me most was Sara's ability to blend art history, neuroscience, and design practice in a single talk. Newton's obsessive personality led to the first color wheel; Goethe challenged his theory with a more emotional approach.

The connection between color theory and frontend development is direct. As an ADSO apprentice, choosing a color palette for a web interface is a UX decision, not a stylistic preference.

Sara's phrase 'every color must generate a conscious, subconscious, and unconscious response' resonated deeply with UX principles. Color accessibility and contrast ratios are technical requirements.

Events like Moda ECCI are essential to professional formation. They remind us that software development happens at the intersection of technology, art, psychology, and culture.

15 key technical terms learned at the event

EnglishSpanishDefinition
Color TheoryTeoría del ColorThe study of how colors generate visual perception, emotion, and meaning in human beings.
PantonePantoneStandardized color identification system used globally in design, fashion, and printing.
SaturationSaturaciónMeasure of color intensity; high saturation means vivid color, low saturation means closer to gray.
HueTono / MatizThe pure attribute of a color that distinguishes it from others — red, blue, green, etc.
SemiologySemiologíaThe study of signs and symbols and their interpretation, applied here to visual color meaning.
Color WheelRueda de ColorCircular diagram representing color relationships, invented by Isaac Newton in 1666.
Complementary ColorsColores ComplementariosColors directly opposite each other on the color wheel that create maximum contrast when paired.
Color HarmonyArmonía CromáticaPleasing arrangement of colors based on their relationships on the color wheel.

CONCLUSION

Technology, Sustainability & Circular Economy

The fashion and creative industries are major contributors to environmental impact. As future technology professionals, we have a responsibility to build digital products that promote sustainability.

♻️
Waste Reduction

Digital platforms enable fashion brands to move from physical catalogs to virtual showrooms, eliminating tons of printed materials.

🔄
Reuse & Circular Design

The circular economy model promotes designing for longevity. In software, this means component-based architecture and design systems.

⚡
Resource Optimization

Optimized code means less server energy consumption. Efficient frontend development reduces the carbon footprint of digital products.

🌿
Sustainable Production

Slow fashion and sustainable design share values with agile software development: iteration, quality, longevity.

🌍
Positive Environmental Impact

Technology can amplify sustainable fashion movements. ADSO skills create real environmental value through ethical digital products.

💡
Final Reflection

Moda ECCI showed me that creativity, technology, and responsibility intersect in the most meaningful work. Color theory is about choices that affect people and planet.
